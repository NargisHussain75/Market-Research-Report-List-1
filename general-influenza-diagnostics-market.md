<p><p>The general influenza diagnostics market includes various testing methods: </p><p>1. Rapid Influenza Diagnostic Tests (RIDT) provide quick results by detecting viral antigens but may lack sensitivity.</p><p>2. Viral culture involves growing the virus in a lab for accurate identification, although it requires more time.</p><p>3. Direct Fluorescent Antigen (DFA) testing uses fluorescent antibodies to detect the virus in respiratory specimens, offering moderate speed and accuracy.</p><p>4. Serological assays assess antibodies in the blood to confirm previous infections, aiding epidemiological studies.</p></p>
