<p><ul><li>Active Exoskeletons</li><li>Passive Exoskeletons</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bionic exoskeletons market is divided into two main types: active and passive exoskeletons. Active exoskeletons are powered devices that use motors and sensors to assist users in movement, enhancing strength and endurance, making them ideal for rehabilitation and industrial applications. In contrast, passive exoskeletons rely on mechanical frameworks and materials without powered components, providing support and reducing fatigue during tasks without external energy, often used in ergonomics and physical labor settings to improve safety and efficiency.</p></p>
