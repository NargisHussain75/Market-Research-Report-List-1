<p><p>The venous thromboembolism treatment market is primarily categorized into two types: Deep Venous Thrombosis (DVT) and Pulmonary Embolism (PE). DVT involves blood clots forming in deep veins, often in the legs, requiring anticoagulants and sometimes thrombolytics for management. PE occurs when these clots migrate to the lungs, leading to serious complications. Treatments for PE may include anticoagulants, thrombolytics, and surgical interventions. Both conditions necessitate prompt diagnosis and tailored therapeutic strategies to mitigate risks and improve patient outcomes.</p></p>
