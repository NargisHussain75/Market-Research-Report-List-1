<p><p>Diagnostic ultrasound devices are categorized into various types based on their imaging capabilities. </p><p>2D ultrasound provides flat images, primarily used for basic examinations, while 3D ultrasound offers a volumetric view, enhancing visualization of anatomical structures. </p><p>4D ultrasound adds a temporal dimension, displaying moving 3D images in real time. </p><p>Doppler ultrasound specializes in assessing blood flow and can detect abnormalities in the cardiovascular system. Each type serves specific diagnostic purposes, contributing to comprehensive patient care.</p></p>
