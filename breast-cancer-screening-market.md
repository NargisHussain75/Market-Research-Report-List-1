<p><p>The breast cancer screening market encompasses various methods aimed at early detection. Mammography screening, the most common, uses X-rays to detect abnormalities in breast tissue. Breast MRI screening employs magnetic fields for detailed imaging, particularly useful for high-risk women or those with dense breast tissue. Breast ultrasound screening uses sound waves to distinguish between cysts and tumors, often as a supplemental tool alongside mammography. Each method plays a vital role in comprehensive breast cancer detection and management strategies.</p></p>
