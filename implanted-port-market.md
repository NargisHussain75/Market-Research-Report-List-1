<p><p>The implanted port market comprises primarily two types: titanium and plastic infusion ports. Titanium infusion ports are known for their durability, biocompatibility, and resistance to corrosion, making them suitable for long-term use in patients requiring frequent venous access. In contrast, plastic infusion ports are generally lighter and less costly, often favored for shorter treatment durations. Both types aim to enhance patient comfort and accessibility for chemotherapy, blood draws, and other medical treatments, catering to diverse clinical needs and preferences.</p></p>
