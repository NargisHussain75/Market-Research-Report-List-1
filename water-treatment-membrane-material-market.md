<p><p>The water treatment membrane material market encompasses various types of membranes, each serving distinct filtration purposes. Reverse Osmosis Membranes effectively remove dissolved solids and contaminants, making them ideal for desalination and potable water production. Ultrafiltration Membranes target larger particles and bacteria, widely used in wastewater treatment. Microfiltration Membranes handle even larger particles and are often employed in pre-treatment processes. Nanofiltration Membranes bridge the gap between ultrafiltration and reverse osmosis, selectively removing divalent ions while allowing monovalent ions to pass. Together, these membranes address diverse water purification needs.</p></p>
