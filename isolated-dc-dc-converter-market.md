<p><p>The isolated DC-DC converter market comprises various topologies, primarily including flyback and forward converters. Flyback converters use a transformer for isolation and energy storage, making them suitable for low to medium power applications. They are known for simplicity and cost-effectiveness. In contrast, forward converters also employ a transformer but transfer energy directly to the output during each switching cycle, offering higher efficiency and better performance in higher power applications. Both types are essential for applications requiring voltage regulation and electrical isolation.</p></p>
