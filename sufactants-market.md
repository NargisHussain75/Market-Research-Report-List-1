<p><p>Surfactants are categorized based on their charge properties into four main types: cationic, anionic, nonionic, and amphoteric surfactants. Cationic surfactants carry a positive charge, making them effective in antimicrobial applications. Anionic surfactants have a negative charge and are widely used in detergents for their excellent cleaning properties. Nonionic surfactants, with no charge, are known for their mildness and versatility in various formulations. Amphoteric surfactants can act as both cationic and anionic, offering unique benefits in diverse applications.</p></p>
