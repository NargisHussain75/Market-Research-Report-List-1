<p><ul><li>Partially Hydrogenated</li><li>Fully Hydrogenated</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Hydrogenated Cottonseed Oil market is categorized into two main types: partially hydrogenated and fully hydrogenated. Partially hydrogenated cottonseed oil contains trans fats, making it more stable for baking and frying but associated with health concerns. In contrast, fully hydrogenated cottonseed oil undergoes complete hydrogenation, eliminating trans fats and resulting in a more saturated fat content. This type is often used in products where a more stable, solid fat is required, catering to health-conscious consumers and food regulations.</p></p>
