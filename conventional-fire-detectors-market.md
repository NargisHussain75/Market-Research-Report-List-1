<p><p>The conventional fire detectors market comprises various types, each serving distinct purposes. Heat detectors respond to temperature changes, ideal for environments where smoke may not be present. Ionization detectors sense smoke particles, effectively detecting fast-flaming fires. Photoelectric detectors utilize light beams to spot smoldering fires. Ionization/photoelectric detectors combine both technologies for enhanced sensitivity. Additionally, other market segments may include specialized detectors for unique applications, such as gas detection or combination alarms, catering to specific safety needs.</p></p>
