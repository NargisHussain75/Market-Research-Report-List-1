<p><p>The turbofan engine market encompasses three main types: low-bypass, high-bypass, and afterburning turbofans. Low-bypass turbofans are typically used in military aircraft, providing high thrust and speed. High-bypass turbofans are prevalent in commercial aviation, offering fuel efficiency and quieter operation for long-haul flights. Afterburning turbofans, often found in fighter jets, enhance thrust by injecting additional fuel in the exhaust, facilitating supersonic flight. Each type serves distinct applications, influencing aircraft design and performance characteristics.</p></p>
