<p><p>The SmFeN bonded magnets market is primarily divided into two types: isotropic and anisotropic. Isotropic magnets exhibit uniform magnetic properties in all directions, making them easier to manufacture and align, but generally offer lower performance. In contrast, anisotropic magnets are engineered to have enhanced magnetic strength in specific directions, resulting in higher performance levels but requiring more complex production processes. The choice between these types depends on the specific application needs, with anisotropic magnets being preferred for high-power applications.</p></p>
