<p><p>The bone grafting screw market encompasses three main types: stainless steel, titanium, and bioabsorbable screws. Stainless steel screws are known for their strength and affordability, making them widely used in various orthopedic procedures. Titanium screws offer superior biocompatibility and corrosion resistance, making them ideal for long-term implants. Bioabsorbable screws, on the other hand, gradually dissolve in the body, eliminating the need for a second surgery and minimizing long-term complications, appealing to both surgeons and patients for less invasive options.</p></p>
