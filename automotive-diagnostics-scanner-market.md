<p><p>The automotive diagnostics scanner market is divided into corded and cordless (Bluetooth) types. Corded scanners connect directly to the vehicle's diagnostic port via a cable, offering reliable connectivity and power without battery concerns. In contrast, cordless scanners leverage Bluetooth technology, allowing for wireless communication with devices and greater mobility during inspections. Both types provide essential vehicle data analysis, but the choice often depends on user preferences for convenience and ease of access in various diagnostic environments.</p></p>
