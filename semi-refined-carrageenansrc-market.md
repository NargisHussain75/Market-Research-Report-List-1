<p><p>Semi-Refined Carrageenan (SRC) is a seaweed-derived hydrocolloid used primarily as a thickening and gelling agent in various food applications. The market is categorized into three types: Kappa Carrageenan, known for forming strong gels in the presence of potassium ions; Iota Carrageenan, which creates softer gels with calcium ions; and Lambda Carrageenan, primarily used for thickening without gel formation. Each type serves distinct functional purposes in food products, dairy, and pharmaceuticals, influencing market demand and application profiles.</p></p>
