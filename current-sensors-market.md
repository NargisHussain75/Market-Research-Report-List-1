<p><p>The current sensors market comprises various types, each with unique features. Hall-effect current sensors utilize magnetic fields to detect current, offering non-intrusive measurement. Shunt current sensors rely on a resistor placed in the current path, providing accurate measurements but require calibration. TMR (Tunneling Magnetoresistance) current sensors leverage advanced materials for high sensitivity and precision in detecting low currents. Other current sensor technologies include those based on Rogowski coils and fiber optics, catering to specific industrial applications and requirements.</p></p>
