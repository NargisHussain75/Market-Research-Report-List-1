<p><p>Marine thermal imaging cameras are essential for maritime applications, classified into two main types: uncooled and cooled. Uncooled thermal cameras use a detector that operates at standard temperatures without the need for cooling, making them compact, lightweight, and cost-effective. In contrast, cooled thermal cameras utilize cryogenic technology to enhance sensitivity and image quality in challenging conditions, resulting in superior performance but at a higher cost. Both types serve vital roles in navigation, security, and surveillance in marine environments.</p></p>
