<p><p>Sacral Nerve Stimulation (SNS) devices are classified into implantable and external devices. Implantable SNS devices involve surgically placed electrodes that stimulate the sacral nerves to treat conditions like urinary incontinence and pelvic pain. These devices are designed for long-term use, providing ongoing therapy. External SNS devices, on the other hand, are non-invasive and typically used for temporary relief or as trial devices prior to implantation. They offer flexibility and ease of use without the commitment of surgery.</p></p>
